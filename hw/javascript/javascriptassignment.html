The orizuru (折鶴 ori- "folded," tsuru "crane"), or paper crane, is a design that is considered to be the most classic of all Japanese origami. It is a representation of the Japanese red-crowned crane that is referred to as the "Honourable Lord Crane" in Japanese culture. If you are able to fold 1000 of them, an ancient Japanese legend promises that you will be granted a wish by the gods.

Steps

Hover over each step for pictures.

  1. Get a blank square sheet of paper.
  2. Fold the corner to meet the other corner to make a triangle.
  3. Fold the triangle in half.
  4. Open up one of the triangles.
  5. Fold it down to make a square.
  6. Do the same for the other side.
  7. Fold the right flap and left flap towards the center, so that the paper edge lines up with the center crease.
  8. Fold the top towards yourself.
  9. Unfold back to the square.
  10. Open up the square and create a diamond shape using the creases you just made as guidelines.
  11. Do the same for the other side.
  12. Fold towards the center.
  13. Repeat on the other side as well.
  14. Open up the side to fold the tip inwards and upwards.
  15. Fold inwards.
  16. Do the same for the other side.
  17. Fold the tip inwards to make the head.
  18. Gently pull open the wings, and you’re finished!
